# Provenance: SwiftTutor Apprentice learning-optimization research

Date: 2026-07-10

## Method

The local `deep-research` skill was selected, but its referenced `prompts/deepresearch.md` file was absent from the installed package. The investigation therefore used the intended structure manually:

- a primary researcher for multimedia and animation evidence;
- a primary researcher for programming-learning evidence;
- an adversarial verifier for counterevidence and boundary conditions;
- an independent root-agent web search and synthesis;
- preference for peer-reviewed experiments, systematic reviews, meta-analyses, official standards, and authoritative curriculum sources.

Three research agents were asked to browse independently. One initially hit model capacity and was retried. All three eventually returned bounded findings. No agent edited repository files.

## Search angles

1. Multimedia segmenting, signaling, learner control, captions, narration, pretraining, and animation boundary conditions.
2. Video-first versus active programming practice.
3. PRIMM, worked examples, Parsons problems, self-explanation, debugging, feedback, retrieval, spacing, and mastery.
4. Beginner HTML/CSS/JavaScript sequencing and Web curriculum conventions.
5. AI-assisted novice programming, metacognition, knowledge gain, generated-code review, and repair.
6. Current official certification objectives for Swift, Web, Cybersecurity, and Networking.
7. Course dashboards, gamification, adaptivity, autoplay, accessibility, and claims likely to fail adversarial review.

## Counterevidence retained

- Multimedia design changes are weaker and less consistent than active-learning interventions.
- Animation has only a small average advantage and works best for inherently dynamic content.
- Learner-control availability alone has near-zero average benefit.
- No direct evidence supports one-time auto-opening or autoplay as a learning intervention.
- Course dashboards can change behavior without improving examination performance.
- HTML → CSS → JavaScript is a plausible dependency order, not a proven universal optimum.
- Gamification effects are heterogeneous; leaderboards and badges can harm motivation or performance.
- Adaptive review has stronger evidence for discrete retention than programming transfer.
- GenAI can improve novice task completion without reliably improving conceptual knowledge.
- Some novices use GenAI productively while others compound metacognitive difficulties, so access alone is not an intervention.
- Certification blueprints define exam coverage, not deep mastery or guaranteed outcomes.

## Excluded or downgraded evidence

- Blog posts and vendor marketing without study methods.
- Engagement-only video studies treated as mastery evidence.
- Reddit anecdotes and unsourced learning advice.
- “Learning styles” and modality-matching claims.
- Exact video-length prescriptions without stable causal evidence.
- Exact spaced-review schedules claimed to be proven for programming.
- Vendor certification marketing treated as evidence of learning effectiveness.
- Studies whose accessible page did not expose enough method or outcome detail for a product claim.

## Known gaps

- No head-to-head trial establishes the optimal HTML/CSS/JavaScript order.
- No direct trial tests a first-visit, default-paused embedded animated Swift lesson.
- Course Home is a UX hypothesis and needs usability testing.
- Programming-specific delayed retrieval and mastery evidence is smaller than the broader cognitive-science literature.
- Several programming studies use small or non-random samples, short interventions, or bundled techniques.
- AI-assisted programming evidence is young; the strongest directly relevant studies still include small observational samples and recent preprints.
- No current study proves that the proposed Understand AI Code thread transfers to maintenance of a learner's own large AI-generated project.
- Certification objectives can change after publication and therefore require periodic version review.

## Interpretation policy

The research brief separates evidence from product inference. Effect sizes are included only where the accessible source or research reviewer supplied them. Product choices labeled as hypotheses require local usability or learning validation rather than being presented as proven outcomes.
